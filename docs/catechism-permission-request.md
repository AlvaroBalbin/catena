# Permission request: Catechism of the Catholic Church (draft)

This is a draft letter to the USCCB for permission to include the Catechism in
Catena as an open, non-commercial, machine-readable corpus. Until permission is
granted **in writing**, the Catechism is NOT redistributed in this repository
(see [`../data/SOURCES.md`](../data/SOURCES.md)).

Send to: USCCB Permissions / Office of the Catechism
(permissions contact via https://www.usccb.org/committees/catechism/use-catechism-catholic-church)

---

Subject: Permission request - open, non-commercial machine-readable use of the Catechism of the Catholic Church

To the USCCB Permissions Office,

I am writing to request written permission regarding the Catechism of the Catholic
Church (English translation, © United States Catholic Conference - Libreria Editrice
Vaticana).

**Who we are.** Catena is a free, open-source project that assembles carefully
sourced Catholic texts into a clean, machine-readable form so that software - in
particular AI question-answering tools - can answer *only* from the actual text of
the sources and *always cite* them, rather than paraphrasing or inventing Church
teaching. Our explicit purpose is fidelity: to make it hard for a program to
misrepresent the Church, by binding every answer to a verbatim, citable source.

**What we are asking to do.** We would like to include the English text of the
Catechism, structured by its own paragraph numbers, so that a passage can be
retrieved and displayed exactly as written, attributed to its paragraph. Concretely
we request permission to:

1. Redistribute the English Catechism text in a structured, paragraph-addressable
   data format within our free, open-source, non-commercial repository; and
2. Display verbatim paragraphs, with citation, in a free retrieval tool that shows
   the reader the exact source text.

**Our commitments.** We would, on every copy and distribution:

- carry the full required copyright notice ("Copyright © [year], United States
  Catholic Conference, Inc. - Libreria Editrice Vaticana. All rights reserved.");
- preserve the text verbatim and unaltered, paragraph-numbered, with no
  paraphrase, abridgement, or commentary mixed into the text;
- keep the use strictly non-commercial and free of charge to the public;
- restrict any distribution terms exactly as you direct, and cease distribution
  promptly on request.

We are aware that the USCCB permits use of fewer than 5,000 words without prior
permission, and that use above that threshold made available to the public at no
cost requires written permission. This letter is that request. Absent your
permission, we will limit ourselves to the under-5,000-word allowance with the
required notice, and otherwise reference the Catechism only by paragraph number.

We would be grateful for your guidance on the terms under which the above would be
acceptable, and we will conform the project to them exactly.

With respect and gratitude for your stewardship of the text,

Alvaro Balbin
[contact]
Catena - [repository URL]

---

## Fallback if permission is not granted

We already comply with this and it ships regardless of the letter's outcome:

- **Never** redistribute the Catechism's full text.
- Reference the Catechism by **paragraph number and structure only** (facts, not
  the copyrighted expression), which lets a tool point a reader to "CCC 1731"
  without reproducing it.
- Quote **individual paragraphs** only within the under-5,000-word allowance, each
  carrying the required copyright notice.
